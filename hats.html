Game Rules
Six Thinking Hats + Royal Hat · AI Readiness Debrief Session
1
One hat at a time
The whole group thinks with the same hat at the same time. When the Royal hat calls a hat, everyone switches — no one stays in their own hat while others move on.
2
Play the role fully
Even if your hat is not your natural style, commit to it. If you are wearing the Yellow hat, find the positives — even if you are sceptical by nature. The constraint unlocks new thinking.
3
No cross-hat debating
When you are in White hat mode, no Red hat reactions. When in Green hat, no Black hat criticism. Save it for when that hat is called. The Royal hat enforces this.
4
Royal hat is the referee
The Royal hat person does not have their own opinion during the discussion — they manage the process. If the Royal hat says "we are moving to Green hat now," the group moves.
5
Every hat gets equal time
The Royal hat keeps time. No single hat dominates the session. If Black hat is getting too much airtime, the Royal hat moves the group on.
6
Close with a cross-group share
At the end of your group discussion, each group shares their top 2–3 insights with the whole room. Listen actively to other groups — their perspective from a different mix of hats will expand your own thinking.
